# SOC Overview

## Today I Learned:

---

## CISO (Chief Information Security Officer) 🤴
The person responsible for making key cybersecurity decisions.

---

## GRC (Governance, Risk & Compliance) 👮
GRC is may be not an engineer, he do the law and government side.

> GRC for money

---

## Blue Team 🧿
Blue team is the common name for roles like SOC analysts and SOC engineers, responsible for protecting and monitoring systems.

---

## SOC Layers

- **L1 Analysts:** Junior members who triage alerts and pass complex cases to L2
> SOC L1 for triage the alert

- **L2 Analysts:** Experienced members who investigate more advanced attacks
> SOC L2 for deep analysis

- **Engineers:** Experts in configuring security tools like EDR or SIEM
> SOC Engineer investigate the issue

- **Manager:** A person who manages the whole SOC team

> I don't know if one person can do all that 🧐 , that will be hard and amazing

> SOC works 24/7 always work 🫠

---

## CIRT (Cyber Incident Response Team)
A specialized team called when an attack is too complex for the SOC to handle alone.
They focus on containing the damage, investigating the root cause, and recovering the systems.

---

## MSSP (Managed Security Services Provider)
An external company that provides security monitoring and management instead of building an internal SOC.
Small and medium companies often use MSSP because building an internal SOC is expensive.

---

## Threat Researcher 🔍
Studies and analyzes hacker groups, their tools, and tactics to understand how they operate.

---

## Penetration Tester ⚔️
Actively attacks systems (with permission) to find vulnerabilities before real hackers do.
